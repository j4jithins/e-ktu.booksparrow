POWER SYSTEM ANALYSIS



Syllabus

Per unit quantities - modeling of power system components - methods of analyzing faults in symmetrical and unsymmetrical case - load flow studies - Automatic Generation Control - Automatic voltage control – Economic load dispatch - Unit commitment - Power system stability - Solution of swing equation - Methods of improving stability limits


  Module I
15%
Per unit quantities-single phase and three phase-selection of base quantities -advantages of per unit system –changing the base of per unit quantities-Simple problems.
Modelling of power system components - single line diagram – per unit quantities. Symmetrical components- sequence impedances and sequence networks of generators, transformers and transmission lines.

  Module II
15%
Methods of analyzing faults in symmetrical and unsymmetrical case- effects of faults - Power system faults - symmetrical faults - short circuit MVA - current limiting reactors - Unsymmetrical faults - single line to ground, line to line, double line to ground faults -consideration of prefault current- problems.
FIRST INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module III
15%
Load flow studies – Introduction-types-network model formulation - formation of bus impedance and admittance matrix, Gauss-Siedel (two iterations), Newton-Raphson (Qualitative analysis only) and Fast Decoupled method (two iterations) - principle of DC load flow.

  Module IV
15%
Automatic Generation Control: Load frequency control: single area and two area systems - Automatic voltage control.
SECOND INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module V
20%
Economic Operation - Distribution of load between units within a plant - transmission loss as a function of plant generation - distribution of load between plants - Method of computing penalty factors and loss coefficients.
Unit commitment: Introduction — Constraints on unit commitments: Spinning reserve, Thermal unit constraints- Hydro constraints. -

  Module VI
20%
Power system stability - steady state, dynamic and transient stability-power angle curve-steady state stability limit
Mechanics of angular motion-Swing equation – Solution of swing equation - Point by Point method - RK method - Equal area criterion application - Methods of improving stability limits.
END SEMESTER EXAM


Course Objectives
  1. To enable the students to analyse power systems under normal and abnormal conditions.
  2. To understand the need for load flow analysis and different methods
  3. To understand power system modeling
  4. To understand the need for stability studies and their analysis


Expected Outcome
The students will be able to:
  1. Analyse power systems under normal and abnormal conditions.
  2. Carry out load flow studies under normal and abnormal conditions

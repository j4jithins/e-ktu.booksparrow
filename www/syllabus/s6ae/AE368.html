PLASTIC ENGINEERING



Syllabus

Engineering Plastics- Concept of testing & identification of plastics- Test methods and standards for bio-degradable plastics - Recycling technologies for bio degradable plastics - Inspection and quality control of moulds - Environmental consideration


  Module I
15%
Sources and Manufacture of raw materials, Methods of Manufacture of Polymer, General Properties and applications of Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene -Polyamides (PA-6,PA-66,PA-6,10,PA-11&12) – Polycarbonates – Poly acetal & Copolymers - Thermoplastic Polyesters (PET&PBT) Poly phenylene oxide – Poly sulfones Fluoropolymers (PVF,PVDF,PTFE,PCTFE) - Thermoplastic Polyurethane.

  Module II
15%
Concept of testing & identification of plastics : Basic concepts of testing - Specification and Standards - National and International Standards - Test specimen preparation - Pre-conditioning and test atmosphere. Identification of plastics by simple tests - Visual examination - Density - Melting point - Solubility test - Flame test - Chemical tests.
FIRST INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module III
15%
Test methods and standards for bio-degradable plastics: Plastics – criteria used in evaluation of biodegradable plastics – description of current Test methods – Scanning test for ready biodegradability – Test for inherent biodegradability – Test for simulation studies – Other methods for assessing polymer biodegradability

  Module IV
15%
Recycling technologies for bio degradable plastics: Conventional recycling – Degradable complicate recycling – reprocessing polyethylene starch/film scrap – Economics in in-plant recycling
SECOND INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module V
20%
Inspection and quality control of moulds : Introduction to Tool Room measuring instruments – Vernier– Micrometer – Height Gauge–Slip Gauge–Dial Gauge–Measuring tapers and angles–CMM

  Module VI
20%
Environmental consideration: Plastic waste – Classification, Segregation, Sorting and Waste Management viz. source reduction, reuse/repair, recycling related to packaging films and constrainers. Pollutants an outline – Chloro Fluoro Carbon (CFC), Dioxin Life cycle assessment: A case study
END SEMESTER EXAM


Course Objectives
1. To give the concept of plastic engineering and their standards.
2. To understand the diverse technological and functional approaches and applications.
3. To provide an insight of testing, identification and quality control.

Expected Outcome
After the successful completion of this course, the students will be able to
  1. become familiar with testing methods and standards of plastic
  2. be able to test the quality control of different modules
  3. be able to identify how to engineer along with the environmental consideration

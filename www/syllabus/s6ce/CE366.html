TRAFFIC ENGINEERING AND MANAGEMENT



Syllabus

Scope and objective of traffic engineering and management, Traffic regulation rules, Highway capacity and introduction to 2010 manual, Design of at grade, grade separated, rotary and signals, traffic safety , influencing factors and preventive measures for traffic accidents, basic diagrams of traffic flow theory, introduction to car following and queuing.


  Module I
15%
Traffic management – scope of traffic management measures – restrictions to turning movements – one way streets – tidal flow operations-Traffic segregation –Traffic calming- Exclusive bus lanes, Introduction to ITS

  Module II
15%
Regulation of traffic – Need and scope of traffic regulations- Motor Vehicle Act – Speed limit at different locations- regulation of the vehicle – regulations concerning the driver rules of the road enforcement
FIRST INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module III
15%
Highway capacity: Its importance in transportation studies – basic, possible and practical capacity – determination of theoretical maximum capacity -passenger car units – level of service – concept in HC manual – factors affecting level of service.

  Module IV
15%
Design of Intersection: Design of at grade & grade separated intersection – rotary intersection – capacity of rotary intersection – traffic signals – warrants of traffic signals,-types of signals, signal coordination, design of fixed time signal –Websters approach
SECOND INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module V
20%
Traffic Safety: causes of road accidents – collection of accident data – influence of road, the vehicle .the driver, the weather and other factors on road accident – preventive measures

  Module VI
20%
Traffic Flow: theory of traffic flow – scope – definition and basic diagrams of traffic flow- basic concepts of light hill – Whitham’s theory – Introduction to Car ‘following theory and queuing’
END SEMESTER EXAM


Course Objectives
1.To set a solid and firm foundation in traffic engineering management, traffic regulation, highway capacity, design of introduction and traffic flow theory concepts.

Expected Outcome
  1. This course will enable students to learn advanced topics in traffic engineering and management.

Digital Electronics and Logic Design



Syllabus

Review of Number Systems and Codes, Digital Logic, Combinational Logic Circuits, Data Processing Circuits, Arithmetic Circuits, Flip-Flops, Registers, Counters, DACs and ADCs, Design of synchronous Sequential Circuits, Introduction to HDL.


  Module I
15%
Number Systems and Codes : Binary, Octal and hexadecimal conversions- ASCII code, Excess -3 code, Gray code, Error detection and correction - Parity generators and checkers - Fixed point and floating point arithmetic. Binary addition and subtraction, unsigned and signed numbers, 1's complement and 2's complement arithmetic.

  Module II
15%
TTL logic and CMOS logic - Logic gates, Universal gates - Boolean Laws and theorems, Sum of Products method, Product of Sum method - K map representation and simplification(upto four variables) - Pairs, Quads, Octets, Dont care conditions.
FIRST INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module III
15%
Combinational circuits: Adders _ Full adder and half adder - Subtractors, halfsubtractor and fullsubtractor - Carry Look ahead adders - ALU(block diagram only). Multiplexers, Demultiplexers, Encoders, BCD to decimel decoders.

  Module IV
15%
Sequential circuits: Flip-Flops, SR, JK, D and T flip-flops, JK Master Slave Flip-flop, Conversion of flip-flops, Registers -SISO,SIPO, PISO, PIPO. Counters : Asynchronous Counters - Modulus of a counter - Mod N counters.
SECOND INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module V
20%
Synchronous counters: Preset and clear modes, Counter Synthesis: Ring counter, Johnson Counter, Mod N counter, Decade counter. State Machines: State transition diagram, Moore and Mealy Machines - Design equation and circuit diagram.

  Module VI
20%
Digital to Analog conversion - R-2R ladder, weighted resistors. Analog to Digital Conversion - Flash ADC, Successive approximation, Integrating ADC. Memory Basics, Read and Write, Addressing, ROMs, PROMs and EPROMs, RAMs, Sequential Programmable Logic Devices - PAL, PLA, FPGA (Introduction and basic concepts only) Introduction to VHDL, Implementation of AND, OR, half adder and full adder.
END SEMESTER EXAM


Course Objectives
To impart knowledge about digital logic and to gain the ability to design various digital circuits

Expected Outcome
After the successful completion of this course, the students will be able to
  1. Familiar with various number systems and Boolean algebra
  2. design and analyse any digital logic gate circuits and Flip flop based systems.
  3. Familiar with combinational circuits
  4. gain the capability of implementing various counters,
  5. describe the operation of ADC and DAC circuits
  6. acquire basic knowledge on VHDL

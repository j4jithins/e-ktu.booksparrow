STRUCTURAL ANALYSIS -11



Syllabus

Slope Deflection Method, Moment Distribution Method, Clapeyrons Theorem (Three Moment Equation) , Kani’s method of analysis, Beams curved in Plan, Plastic Theory.


  Module I
15%
Clapeyrons Theorem (Three Moment Equation) :Derivation of three moment equation - application of three moment equation for analysis of continuous beams under the effect of applied loads and uneven support settlement.

  Module II
15%
Slope Deflection Method : Analysis of continuous beams- beams with overhang- analysis of rigid frames - frames without sway and with sway - different types of loads -settlement effects.
FIRST INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module III
15%
Moment Distribution Method:- Moment Distribution method – analysis of beams and frames – non sway and sway analysis .

  Module IV
15%
Kani’s Method: Kani’s Method of analysis applied to continuous beams and single bay single storey rigid frames rigid frames – frames without sway and with sway.
SECOND INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module V
20%
Beams curved in plan: Analysis of cantilever beam curved in plan, analysis of circular beams over simple supports.

  Module VI
20%
Plastic Theory: Introduction – plastic hinge concepts – plastic modulus – shape factor – redistribution of moments – collapse mechanisms – Plastic analysis of beams and portal frames by equilibrium and mechanism methods.(Single Storey and Single bay Frames only)
END SEMESTER EXAM


Course Objectives
1. To equip the students with the force and displacement methods of structural analysis with emphasis on analysis of rigid frames and trusses.

Expected Outcome
  1. analyse structures using force method
  2. analyse structures using displacement method
  3. analyse curved beams in plan
  4. analyse structures using plastic theory

QUESTION PAPER PATTERN (End semester exam)

Maximum Marks: 100
Exam Duration: 3 Hrs
The question paper shall have three parts.
Part A -Module I & II : Answer 2 questions out of 3 questions (15 marks each)
Part B - Module III & IV: Answer 2 questions out of 3 questions (15 marks each)
Part C - Module V & VI: Answer 2 questions out of 3 questions (20 marks each)
Note: 1.Each part should uniformly cover the two modules in that part. 2. Each question can have a maximum of 4 subdivisions (a,b,c,d), if needed.

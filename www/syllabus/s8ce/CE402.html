ENVIRONMENTAL ENGINEERING – II



Syllabus

Wastewater, sources, characteristics, oxygen demand Design of sewers, Circular sewers, Partial flow and full flow conditions. Sewer appurtenances, Disposal of wastewater, Streeter Phelps equation, Oxygen sag curve, Treatment methods, Aerobic and anaerobic methods, Design of various treatment units-Screening, Grit chamber, Sedimentation tank, Activated Sludge process, Trickling filter, Rotating biological contactor, Septic tanks, Imhoff tanks, Oxidation ditches, Oxidation ponds, Upflow anaerobic sludge blanket reactors, Sludge digestion, Sludge drying bed.


  Module I
15%
Wastewater- Sources and flow rates, Domestic wastewater, Estimation of quantity of wastewater, Dry weather flow, storm water flow, Time of concentration
Sewers, Design of circular sewers under full and partial flow conditions

  Module II
15%
Sewer appurtenances-Man holes, Catch basin, flushing devices, Inverted siphon. Ventilation of sewers.
Sewage, Sewerage, Systems of sewerage
Sewage characteristics- Physical, chemical and biological parameters, Biological oxygen demand, first stage BOD, Chemical oxygen demand, Relative stability, Population equivalent.
FIRST INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module III
15%
Waste water disposal systems- Self purification of streams, Dilution -Oxygen sag curve, Streeter Phelp’s Equation, land treatment
Treatment of sewage-Preliminary and Primary treatment -Theory and design of Screen, Grit chamber, Detritus chamber, Flow equalization tank and Sedimentation tank.

  Module IV
15%
Secondary treatment methods-Contact bed, Intermittent sand filter, Theory and design of Trickling filter, Activated sludge process, Trickling filter-High rate, standard. Rotating biological contactor
SECOND INTERNAL EXAMINATION

  Module V
20%
Design of Septic tank and Imhoff tank,
Principle and working of Oxidation ditch and oxidation ponds. Aerated lagoons, Design of upflow anaerobic sludge blanket reactors

  Module VI
20%
Sludge treatment and disposal-Methods of thickening, Sludge digestion- Anaerobic digestion, Design of sludge digestion tanks and Sludge drying beds, methods of sludge disposal
END SEMESTER EXAM


Course Objectives
  1. To understand the various sources and characteristics of wastewater
  2. To know the various treatment methods available for wastewater treatment


Expected Outcome
The students will be able to:
  1. have an understanding of the various types of treatment methods for wastewater
  2. know the design aspects of various treatment units in a wastewater treatment plant.
